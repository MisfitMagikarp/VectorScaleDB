Finance

Financial Markets Intelligence

Detect market regimes in real time, track cross-asset correlation shifts, replay historical patterns sorted by similarity, and compress tick data through behavioral regime detection. Purpose-built for quantitative workflows.

Market Regime Detection

Automatically classify market conditions into behavioral regimes. No manual labeling, no lagging indicators.

Regimes
Four Core Regime Types
VectorScaleDB's drift-based regime detection classifies market behavior into four regimes: LowVol (compressed, range-bound), HighVol (expanded volatility, crisis risk), Trending (directional momentum), and MeanReverting (oscillating around equilibrium). Transitions between regimes are detected as change-points in the market's behavioral trajectory.
Confidence
Transition Confidence Scoring
Every regime transition carries a confidence score (0.0-1.0) derived from the magnitude of behavioral drift. A sudden volatility expansion (LowVol to HighVol with 0.97 confidence) triggers differently than a gradual drift (LowVol to Trending with 0.62 confidence). Configure webhook alerts on transition events above your confidence threshold.

Cross-Asset Correlation Tracking

Monitor correlation matrices across asset classes in real time. Detect correlation regime shifts before they cascade through your portfolio.

Tracking
Rolling Correlation Matrices
Track pairwise correlations across any set of entities (equities, bonds, commodities, FX, crypto) using configurable rolling windows. Each correlation pair is itself a temporal vector, enabling similarity search on correlation patterns: "when has the SPX-TLT correlation behaved like this before?"
Detection
Correlation Shift Detection
Correlation regime breaks are detected through the same drift-based mechanism as market regimes. When the SPX-GLD correlation shifts from -0.3 to +0.4 over two weeks, VectorScaleDB identifies the change-point, timestamps it, and surfaces the contributing factors. Risk models can consume these events in real time.
Alerting
Portfolio Risk Triggers
Configure alerts on correlation events that matter to your book. "Notify when any cross-asset correlation in my universe shifts by more than 0.3 within a 5-day window." Alerts fire through webhooks with full context: the pair, the shift magnitude, the regime transition, and the timestamp.

Historical Pattern Replay

Find the historical periods that most resemble current market conditions. Not chart pattern matching. Behavioral trajectory similarity via FastDTW.

Search
Trajectory Similarity Search
Define a pattern window (e.g., "SPX behavior over the last 7 trading days") and search across years of compressed history. FastDTW handles temporal warping: a pattern that played out over 5 days in 2024 matches a similar pattern that took 8 days in 2019. Results ranked by similarity score with subsequent-period returns attached.
Context
Multi-Asset Pattern Context
Pattern matches include the full cross-asset context at the time of the historical match. "The last time SPX behaved like this, bonds were in a HighVol regime and the dollar was trending." Context spans all entities in your universe, not just the queried instrument.

Tick Data Compression & Adapters

Ingest from FIX feeds and CSV files. Compress tick-level data through behavioral regime detection.

Compression
Tick-Level Regime Detection
Raw tick data (price, bid, ask, volume, spread) is vectorized per symbol. Periods of stable quoting behavior compress into single regime segments. A quiet pre-market session with 50,000 ticks compresses to 3 segments. The opening cross with rapid regime changes retains full temporal resolution.
FIX Protocol
FIX 4.4 / 5.0 Adapter
Native FIX protocol adapter for direct market data feed ingestion. Execution reports (tag 35=8), market data snapshots (tag 35=W), and incremental refreshes (tag 35=X) are parsed into temporal vectors. Supports session-level and application-level message types.
CSV
Adaptive CSV Detection
Point VectorScaleDB at any market data CSV. Column detection automatically identifies symbol, timestamp, price, bid, ask, volume, and derived fields. No schema definition required. Supports millions of rows with streaming ingestion and on-the-fly compression.

Example Workflow

Ingest market data, detect the current regime, and replay similar historical patterns.

# Ingest tick data from a CSV file
curl -X POST http://localhost:8080/v1/ingest/csv \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "source": "/data/market/SPX_ticks_2026Q1.csv",
    "entity_type": "MarketTick",
    "entity_id": "SPX",
    "column_detection": "adaptive",
    "expected_columns": ["timestamp", "price", "bid", "ask", "volume"],
    "compression": {
      "enabled": true
    }
  }'
