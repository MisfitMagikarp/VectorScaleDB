Life Sciences

Genomics & DNA

Temporal-semantic analysis of genomic data. Store DNA foundation model embeddings, track gene expression regimes over time, predict variant effects through embedding similarity, and trace single-cell trajectories through differentiation and drug response.

DNA Foundation Model Embeddings

Store and query high-dimensional embeddings from DNA foundation models. Track how genomic region representations evolve across conditions, treatments, or developmental stages.

Storage
High-Dimensional Embedding Index
Ingest embeddings up to 4,096 dimensions from DNA foundation models like Evo 2, Nucleotide Transformer, and DNABERT-2. VectorScaleDB's HNSW index enables sub-millisecond nearest-neighbor search across millions of genomic region embeddings. Find functionally similar sequences regardless of primary sequence identity.
Temporal
Condition-Dependent Representation Tracking
The same genomic region can have different embeddings under different conditions: treatment vs. control, disease vs. healthy, early development vs. mature tissue. VectorScaleDB stores each embedding as a temporal observation, building a trajectory of how a region's representation shifts across experimental conditions. Regime detection identifies when representations stabilize or diverge.
Search
Cross-Model Embedding Comparison
Compare embeddings from different foundation models for the same genomic regions. Identify where models agree and disagree on functional similarity. VectorScaleDB's trajectory comparison reveals whether model disagreements are systematic (architectural differences) or condition-specific (one model captures treatment effects better).

Gene Expression Temporal Analysis

Regime detection for RNA-seq time series. Automatically classify expression behavior and compress steady-state periods while preserving transition dynamics.

Regimes
Expression Regime Classification
VectorScaleDB's drift-based regime detection classifies gene expression behavior into temporal regimes: Stable (steady-state expression), Upregulated (increasing expression), Downregulated (decreasing expression), Oscillating (periodic cycling), and Bistable (switching between two states). Transitions are detected automatically with timestamp precision and confidence scores.
Compression
1000x Compression on Steady-State Expression
Most genes spend most of their time in stable expression regimes. VectorScaleDB compresses hours or days of steady-state expression data into single regime segments while retaining full temporal resolution during transitions, drug responses, and perturbation events. Store genome-wide expression time courses at a fraction of the raw data cost. Both bulk RNA-seq and single-cell expression data are supported natively.

Variant Effect Prediction

Ingest variant data, find similar variants by embedding similarity, and predict functional impact through behavioral pattern matching across variant databases.

Ingestion
VCF Data Ingestion
Ingest VCF files with automatic parsing of variant annotations, quality scores, and allele frequencies. Each variant becomes a temporal vector capturing its genomic context, predicted structural impact, and population frequency. Batch ingestion handles whole-genome variant call sets with millions of variants.
Search
Embedding Similarity Search
Find variants with similar functional profiles using KNN search on variant embeddings. "Show me all known variants whose genomic context embedding is within distance 0.1 of this novel variant." Results include known pathogenicity classifications, enabling transfer of annotations from characterized to novel variants based on structural and contextual similarity.
Prediction
Behavioral Pattern Matching
Beyond static similarity, VectorScaleDB matches variant effects through behavioral trajectories. If a known pathogenic variant produces a characteristic gene expression regime change (stable to downregulated), and a novel variant produces the same regime transition, the behavioral evidence strengthens the pathogenicity prediction. Pattern replay across variant databases surfaces historical parallels.

Single-Cell Trajectory Analysis

Track cell state evolution through differentiation, drug response, or disease progression. Compare trajectories across cell populations with temporal warping.

Trajectories
Cell State Evolution Tracking
Each cell's gene expression profile becomes a temporal vector that evolves through differentiation stages, drug exposure windows, or disease progression milestones. VectorScaleDB tracks these trajectories natively, detecting regime transitions that mark cell fate decisions, drug response onset, or phenotypic switches. Query "when did this cell population commit to the neural lineage?" directly from compressed expression trajectories.
Comparison
Cross-Population DTW Comparison
Compare differentiation trajectories across cell populations using FastDTW, accounting for temporal offsets in development. A population that differentiates over 7 days in one protocol can be compared to a population that takes 12 days in another. DTW aligns the trajectories, scoring similarity independent of absolute timing. Identify which protocols produce the most consistent differentiation paths and rank populations by trajectory quality.

Cross-Domain Integration

Bridge genomics with bio-computing. Correlate gene expression regimes with neural organoid activity and detect cascade effects between genomic and phenotypic domains.

Cascade
Genomic-Neural Cascade Detection
VectorScaleDB's cross-domain cascade detection identifies when gene expression regime changes precede or follow neural activity shifts. "Does upregulation of ion channel genes correlate with increased synchrony in the organoid 24 hours later?" Cascade analysis quantifies the correlation, estimates time lags, and assigns confidence scores across the genomic and neural domains simultaneously.
Multi-Omics
Unified Multi-Omics Index
Gene expression, DNA embeddings, neural recordings, and organoid behavioral data share the same temporal-semantic index. No separate databases, no ETL pipelines between modalities. A single query can span genomic and phenotypic data: "find organoids where BDNF expression regime shifts correlate with learning onset." The unified index makes multi-omics analysis a query rather than an integration project.
Alerting
Cross-Domain Alerting
Configure alerts that span domains. "Notify when a gene expression regime change in the neurotransmitter pathway is followed by an anomalous neural activity pattern within 48 hours." Alerts fire through webhooks with full context: the expression change, the neural anomaly, the time lag, and the cascade confidence score. Dampening windows prevent alert storms during large-scale perturbation experiments.

Native Genomics Entity Types
GeneExpression GenomicVariant DnaEmbedding CellState
REST
6 Genomics Endpoints
Dedicated REST endpoints for VCF ingestion, expression time-series ingestion, embedding batch upload, variant similarity search, expression regime detection, and single-cell trajectory comparison. All under the standard /v1/ namespace.

GraphQL queries for expression regime history, variant effect prediction, and cell trajectory comparison. Subscriptions for real-time expression regime change notifications during live experiments.

4 MCP Tools
MCP server tools for AI agent integration: ingest genomic data, query expression regimes, search variant embeddings, and run cross-domain cascade analysis. Automate genomics workflows directly from Claude or other MCP-compatible AI agents.
Format
VCF Adapter
Native VCF 4.x parsing with support for multi-sample files, structural variants, and INFO/FORMAT field extraction. Automatic variant vectorization from annotations and quality metrics.
Format
Expression Adapter
Ingest gene expression matrices from CSV, TSV, or H5AD (AnnData) formats. Supports both bulk RNA-seq count matrices and single-cell expression profiles with automatic normalization and vectorization.
Format
Embedding Adapter
Batch ingest pre-computed embeddings from DNA foundation models. Accepts NumPy arrays, JSON vectors, or Arrow columnar format. Metadata tags link embeddings to genomic coordinates and model provenance.

Example Workflow

Ingest gene expression time series, detect expression regimes, and find similar variant effects.

# Ingest gene expression time series from an RNA-seq experiment

  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \

    "entity_type": "GeneExpression",
    "genes": ["BDNF", "NTRK2", "SYN1", "GAD1", "SLC17A7"],
    "timepoints": [

        "timestamp": "2026-03-01T00:00:00Z",
        "label": "baseline",
        "values": {
          "BDNF": [12.4, 11.8, 13.1],
          "NTRK2": [8.2, 7.9, 8.5],
          "SYN1": [45.1, 44.3, 46.2],
          "GAD1": [22.7, 23.1, 22.4],
          "SLC17A7": [31.5, 30.9, 32.1]
        }
      },
      {
        "timestamp": "2026-03-03T00:00:00Z",
        "label": "treatment_48h",
        "values": {
          "BDNF": [28.9, 27.4, 30.2],
